## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Cosmas All Door Locks

Choosing the right door hardware involves more than just picking a style you like. To ensure you get the perfect combination of security, functionality, and aesthetics, consider these key factors before making your purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, identify the function of the door. Is it an exterior door that needs high security, or an interior door that only needs privacy?
*   **Entry Locks:** For front, back, and garage doors. They have a keyed cylinder on the outside and a turn-button or thumb-turn on the inside. They are often paired with a deadbolt.
*   **Deadbolts:** Provide the primary security for exterior doors. Single-cylinder deadbolts use a key outside and a thumb-turn inside. Double-cylinder deadbolts require a key on both sides, which is ideal for doors near glass.
*   **Privacy Locks:** For bedrooms and bathrooms. They have a lock on the inside (push-button or turn-button) but no key on the outside, though they usually have a small hole for emergency access.
*   **Passage Locks:** For closets or hallways. They have no locking mechanism and are used simply to latch a door closed.
*   **Handlesets:** These are decorative, high-security sets for front doors, consisting of a large exterior handle, an interior knob or lever, and a deadbolt.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Cosmas is known for offering excellent value, but prices still vary by function and style. Set a realistic budget for your project. A single privacy lever might cost between $20-$30, while a keyed entry lever could be $25-$40. A full front door handleset can range from $80-$150. Buying in multi-packs, like the Cosmas 80 Series combo pack, can offer significant savings if you are outfitting multiple doors.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that add convenience and ensure compatibility:
*   **Adjustable Latch/Backset:** This is crucial. Most residential doors have a backset (distance from the edge of the door to the center of the bore hole) of either 2 3/8 inches or 2 3/4 inches. An adjustable latch fits both, simplifying installation. All the Cosmas locks on our list have this feature.
*   **Reversible Handing:** This means the lever can be installed on both right-hand and left-hand swinging doors. This universal design removes the guesswork of figuring out your door's "handing."
*   **Keyed Alike:** This service, offered for free by Cosmas, allows you to have multiple locks keyed to operate with a single key. It's incredibly convenient for homeowners.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The best door locks are made from solid metal, not plated plastic. Look for solid brass or zinc construction for durability and a substantial feel. The finish is also important. A high-quality finish like Oil Rubbed Bronze or Satin Nickel should be applied evenly and be resistant to chipping, fading, and corrosion, especially on exterior doors exposed to the elements.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Choosing a reputable brand like Cosmas ensures you are getting a product that has been tested and meets industry standards for security and durability. Established brands also typically offer better customer support and warranties. Cosmas has built a strong reputation for providing stylish, reliable hardware at an accessible price point.

### Installation & Fit - Considerations for space and storage
Before buying, measure your door's thickness, backset, and the diameter of the bore hole to ensure a match. Most modern hardware is designed to fit standard door preparations, but it's always wise to double-check, especially in older homes. A standard door thickness is between 1 3/8 and 1 3/4 inches. All the locks reviewed here are designed to fit these standard dimensions, making installation a straightforward DIY project for most homeowners.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose cosmas all door locks?

Choosing the best Cosmas door locks for your home can be a simple and rewarding process if you follow a few logical steps. This ensures you get the right function, fit, and style for every door.

**Step 1: Inventory Your Doors**
Walk through your home and make a list of every door that needs new hardware. For each door, note its function:
*   Exterior (Front, Back, Garage)
*   Interior Privacy (Bedroom, Bathroom)
*   Interior Passage (Closet, Hallway)

**Step 2: Determine the Function Needed**
Based on your inventory, assign a lock type to each door. Exterior doors will need a keyed entry lock and a deadbolt. Privacy doors need a privacy lock. Passage doors need a non-locking passage knob or lever.

**Step 3: Choose Your Style and Finish**
Decide on a consistent look for your home. Do you prefer the modern, clean lines of the 30 or 50 Series levers, or the classic curves of the 80 Series? Do you want the warm, traditional feel of Oil Rubbed Bronze or the cool, contemporary look of Satin Nickel? Choosing one style and finish for the whole house creates a cohesive, professionally designed feel.

**Step 4: Measure Your Doors**
While most Cosmas locks fit standard doors, it's a quick and crucial check. Measure the backset (2 3/8" or 2 3/4") and the door thickness (usually 1 3/8" to 1 3/4"). This confirms the adjustable latches will work perfectly.

**Step 5: Plan Your Keying Strategy**
For all your exterior doors, decide if you want them keyed alike. Taking advantage of Cosmas's free service is highly recommended. It's a massive convenience to have one key for your front door, back door, and any other entry points.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Buying the Wrong Function:** Don't put a privacy lever on a front door or an entry lock on a bathroom.
*   **Ignoring Handing:** While all the levers on our list are reversible, not all brands are. It's a good habit to be aware of your door's swing direction.
*   **Forgetting the Deadbolt:** A lever or knob alone is not sufficient security for an exterior door. Always pair it with a quality deadbolt.
