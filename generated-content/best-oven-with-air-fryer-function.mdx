### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about how you plan to use the oven. Are you cooking for one or two people, or a large family? This will determine the capacity you need, which can range from a compact 10 quarts to a massive 26 quarts or more. Consider the types of food you cook most often. If you love roasted chicken, a model with a rotisserie function is a must. If you enjoy making jerky or dried fruit, look for a dehydration setting. Match the appliance's capabilities to your culinary habits.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Oven and air fryer combos come in a wide price range. You can find entry-level models from around $100 to $150, which are great for basic air frying and baking. Mid-range options, typically costing between $150 and $250, offer more features, better build quality, and more precise controls. Premium models, like those from Breville, can cost $300 or more, but they provide superior cooking technology, exceptional durability, and a wider array of functions like slow cooking and proofing. Set a budget that aligns with your needs and expectations.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look beyond the function count and examine the core technology. A powerful convection fan (some models have dual-speed fans) is crucial for effective air frying. Digital controls and LCD screens offer more precision than manual dials. Check the temperature range; a wider range (e.g., 95°F to 450°F) allows for more versatility, from dehydrating to searing. Also, consider the included accessories. A good set of racks, baskets, trays, and rotisserie spits adds significant value.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
An oven is an investment, so look for signs of quality construction. A stainless steel body is generally more durable and easier to clean than plastic. Check for a well-insulated door that closes securely to retain heat efficiently. The quality of the interior matters too; a non-stick or stainless steel interior simplifies cleanup. Read reviews to see how the appliance holds up over time, paying attention to comments about the fan, heating elements, and control panel.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with reputable brands like Breville, Cuisinart, Ninja, and Instant Pot often provides a better experience. These companies typically offer better build quality, more reliable performance, and responsive customer service. They also tend to have better warranties, giving you peace of mind. While lesser-known brands can offer good value, established manufacturers have a proven track record of producing quality kitchen appliances.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Countertop space is precious. Before you buy, measure the area where you plan to place the oven, and check the product's dimensions (width, depth, and height). Remember to leave some clearance around the unit for ventilation, as they can generate significant heat. If space is extremely limited, consider innovative designs like the Ninja Flip oven, which can be stored vertically to free up your counter when not in use.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose oven with air fryer function?

Choosing the best oven with an air fryer function requires a methodical approach to ensure you get an appliance that truly meets your needs. Follow these steps for a successful purchase.

**Step 1: Assess Your Kitchen and Lifestyle**
Before looking at any models, evaluate your available counter space and your typical cooking habits. Measure the intended spot. How many people do you cook for? What types of meals do you prepare most often? This initial assessment will immediately narrow down your options based on size and primary functions.

**Step 2: Define Your Must-Have Features**
Create a short list of non-negotiable features. Is a rotisserie essential for you? Do you need a dehydration function? Are precise digital controls more important than simple manual knobs? Prioritizing your needs helps you focus on what truly matters and avoid being swayed by features you'll never use.

**Step 3: Set a Realistic Budget**
Determine how much you are willing to spend. As outlined in the buying guide, prices vary significantly. Having a clear budget helps you filter your search and compare models within a similar price range, ensuring you get the best value for your money.

**Step 4: Research and Compare Top Contenders**
Using your criteria from the previous steps, research specific models. Read expert reviews (like this one!) and, just as importantly, read user reviews on retail sites. Look for patterns in feedback regarding performance, reliability, and ease of cleaning. Compare the specs, accessories, and warranties of your top 2-3 choices.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring Dimensions:** Falling in love with an oven only to find it doesn't fit on your counter is a common and frustrating mistake. Always measure first.
*   **Choosing Based on Function Count Alone:** Don't be dazzled by a model with 24 functions if you'll only ever use five. Focus on the quality of the core functions you need.
*   **Forgetting About Cleanup:** Look for features like a removable crumb tray, non-stick interior, and dishwasher-safe accessories. A difficult-to-clean oven will quickly fall out of use.

**When to Upgrade vs. Budget Options:**
Choose a budget option if you're new to air fryer ovens and want to test the waters, or if you only need basic functionality. Upgrade to a premium model if you are a passionate home cook, rely on your countertop oven for daily meals, and value precision, durability, and advanced features like slow cooking or smart heating technology.
