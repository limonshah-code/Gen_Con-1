## What to Look for Before Buying the Best High End Built In Microwave

Choosing a high-end built-in microwave is a significant decision that impacts your kitchen's functionality and aesthetic. To ensure you make the right choice, consider these critical factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess how you'll use the microwave. Are you primarily reheating leftovers and making popcorn, or do you need advanced features like convection cooking, broiling, or sensor reheating for gourmet meal prep? Consider the size of your typical dishes. A large family might need a capacity of 2.0 cu. ft. or more, while a couple might be fine with 1.2 cu. ft. Also, measure your cabinet cutout precisely. A built-in microwave must fit perfectly, so check the manufacturer's required cutout dimensions (width, height, and depth) against your available space.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
High-end built-in microwaves represent a significant investment. Prices can range from around $500 for a basic but stylish model to over $2,000 for a feature-packed microwave drawer or convection combo. A standard high-quality built-in model typically falls in the $600-$1200 range. A microwave drawer will usually start around $1,200, and a microwave-convection oven combo can range from $800 to $2,500+. Set a realistic budget that includes the cost of a matching trim kit if it's sold separately, as well as potential installation costs.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look beyond the basics. Key features to consider in a high-end model include:
*   **Wattage:** Higher wattage (1000W or more) means faster, more even cooking.
*   **Sensor Cooking:** This feature uses sensors to detect moisture and humidity, automatically adjusting cooking time and power for perfect results.
*   **Inverter Technology:** Unlike traditional microwaves that pulse power, inverter technology delivers a constant, steady stream of energy for more precise cooking, especially at lower power levels.
*   **Convection Cooking:** This feature uses a fan to circulate hot air, allowing you to bake, roast, and brown food, essentially turning your microwave into a second oven.
*   **Drawer vs. Swing Door:** Drawer models are installed under-counter and are great for accessibility and a clean aesthetic. Traditional swing-door models are more common and can be installed in a wall cabinet.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
A high-end appliance should be built to last. Look for a stainless steel exterior and interior, as it's durable, easy to clean, and resists rust and odors. Check for sturdy door construction, especially on drawer models where the mechanism needs to be robust. A soft-close door is a premium feature that indicates better build quality and adds a touch of luxury. Reading user reviews can provide insight into the long-term durability of a specific model.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Stick with well-established brands known for quality and reliability in kitchen appliances. Brands like Panasonic, Frigidaire, KitchenAid, and Bosch have a long history of producing durable products and generally offer better customer service and warranty support. While newer brands can offer innovative features, an established reputation often provides more peace of mind for a long-term investment.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While built-in microwaves aren't portable, their size is a critical consideration. The external dimensions must match your cabinet cutout. Pay close attention to the required depth, as you need adequate clearance for ventilation behind the unit. The internal capacity (measured in cubic feet) determines how much food you can cook at once. Ensure the turntable diameter is large enough for your most-used dinner plates and casserole dishes.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy high end built in microwave?

Investing in a high-end built-in microwave is a decision that pays dividends in both functionality and kitchen aesthetics. While a basic countertop model can certainly reheat coffee, a high-end built-in unit elevates the entire cooking experience. These appliances are designed to integrate seamlessly into your cabinetry, creating a custom, streamlined look that significantly enhances your kitchen's design and can even increase your home's value. Beyond looks, the performance is in a different league. Features like inverter technology provide continuous power for incredibly even cooking, eliminating the dreaded hot-and-cold spots. Sensor cooking takes the guesswork out of preparing vegetables, meats, and leftovers, ensuring perfect results every time.

Compared to cheaper alternatives, the long-term benefits are substantial. High-end models are constructed with superior materials like heavy-gauge stainless steel, ensuring they withstand years of daily use. They often come with more advanced and useful features, such as convection capabilities that allow them to double as a second oven—invaluable for holidays and entertaining. While the initial cost is higher, the investment translates to better-tasting food, a more efficient cooking process, and a durable appliance that complements your home for years to come. It’s a wise decision for anyone who values quality, performance, and sophisticated design in their kitchen.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose high end built in microwave?

Choosing the best high-end built-in microwave involves a methodical approach to ensure it meets your specific needs and fits perfectly into your kitchen. Follow this step-by-step guide to make a confident choice.

**Step 1: Define Your Primary Use Case**
Before looking at models, decide what you need. Will this be your go-to for quick reheats, or do you want to bake, roast, and broil? If you entertain often or have a large family, a convection-microwave combo or a model with a large capacity (over 2.0 cu. ft.) is ideal. If you value a minimalist aesthetic and have under-counter space, a microwave drawer is your best bet.

**Step 2: Measure, Measure, and Measure Again**
This is the most critical step for a built-in appliance. Carefully measure the width, height, and depth of your cabinet cutout. Write these numbers down and compare them to the 'cutout dimensions' listed in the specifications for every model you consider. Remember to account for required clearances for ventilation, usually specified by the manufacturer.

**Step 3: Set Your Feature Priorities**
Create a list of must-have features. For many, sensor cooking is non-negotiable for its convenience. For serious cooks, inverter technology and convection capabilities are top priorities. Other features to consider are a keep-warm function, specific presets you'll use often, and safety features like a child lock.

**Step 4: Research Brands and Read Reviews**
Focus on reputable brands known for kitchen appliances. Once you have a shortlist of models that fit your space and feature requirements, dive into user and professional reviews. Look for patterns in feedback regarding reliability, ease of use, and customer service experiences.

**Step 5: Consider the Final Look**
Ensure the microwave's finish (e.g., stainless steel, black stainless) matches your other appliances. If the microwave requires a separate trim kit for a built-in look, make sure you purchase the correct one and factor its cost into your budget.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring Cutout Dimensions:** Buying a microwave that doesn't fit is a costly and frustrating mistake.
*   **Forgetting the Trim Kit:** Many built-in models look unfinished without the matching trim kit, which is often sold separately.
*   **Paying for Features You Won't Use:** Don't get swayed by a long list of features if you only need basic functionality. Choose a model that aligns with your actual cooking habits.

## FAQ

**What is the best high end built in microwave for beginners?**
For beginners, the best high-end model is one that combines powerful performance with intuitive controls. The Frigidaire PMBS3080A is an excellent choice. Its Sensor Cook and Sensor Reheat functions take the guesswork out of cooking, providing great results automatically. The interface is straightforward, making it easy to use right out of the box without needing to study a complicated manual.

**How much should I spend on high end built in microwave?**
You should expect to spend between $600 and $2,000. A high-quality model with sensor cooking and a sleek design typically costs around $800-$1,200. Microwave drawers and convection combination ovens usually start at $1,200 and can go up from there. Your budget should align with the features you prioritize, such as convection capabilities or an automatic drawer design.

**What features are most important in high end built in microwave?**
The most important features are Sensor Cooking for automated, perfect results; Inverter Technology for even, consistent heating; and a high wattage (1000W+) for speed. For added versatility, a Convection function that allows you to bake and roast is a top-tier feature. Finally, a quality build with a stainless steel interior ensures durability and ease of cleaning.

**How do I maintain my high end built in microwave?**
Maintenance is simple. Wipe down the interior with a damp cloth and mild detergent after any spills to prevent food from baking on. For tougher grime, heat a bowl of water with lemon slices for a few minutes to create steam, which will loosen residue. Regularly clean the exterior with a stainless steel cleaner to maintain its finish. Ensure ventilation openings are kept clear of dust and debris.

**Are expensive high end built in microwave worth it?**
Yes, for those who value performance, design, and durability. Expensive models offer superior cooking technology like inverters and sensors, resulting in better-tasting food. They are also built with higher-quality materials for a longer lifespan and offer a seamless, integrated look that enhances your kitchen's aesthetic. The added functionality, like convection cooking, can also provide the utility of a second oven.

**What brands make the best high end built in microwave?**
Several brands are renowned for their high-end microwaves. Panasonic is a leader in cooking technology with its Cyclonic Inverter systems. Frigidaire, KitchenAid, and Bosch are trusted for their reliability and user-friendly features. Brands like Sharp are pioneers in the microwave drawer category, while companies like KoolMore and thermomate offer versatile convection combination units with premium features.

**How long do high end built in microwave typically last?**
A high-end built-in microwave should last approximately 7 to 10 years, and sometimes longer with proper care. Their superior build quality and more robust components often contribute to a longer lifespan compared to budget countertop models. The magnetron, which is the key cooking component, is typically the part that wears out over time.

**Can I find good high end built in microwave on a budget?**
While 'budget' is relative in the high-end category, you can find excellent value. Look for models like the thermomate 24 Inch Convection Microwave Combo, which offers advanced features like convection and sensor cooking at a more accessible price point. You might trade a premium brand name for more functionality, but you can still get a very capable and stylish built-in appliance.
