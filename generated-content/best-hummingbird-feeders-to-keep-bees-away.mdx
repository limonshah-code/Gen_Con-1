## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Hummingbird Feeders To Keep Bees Away

Choosing the perfect hummingbird feeder involves more than just picking a pretty design. To ensure a bee-free experience for you and your hummingbirds, consider these crucial factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess your specific situation. Do you have a major ant problem, a bee problem, or both? If ants are your main issue, a feeder with a deep, reliable ant moat is essential. If bees are the primary culprits, look for feeders with long, narrow feeding ports that a hummingbird's long beak can reach but a bee's shorter tongue cannot. Saucer-style feeders also naturally deter bees because the nectar level sits below the feeding port. Consider the number of hummingbirds in your area; a high-traffic yard might benefit from a larger capacity feeder or multiple smaller ones.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Hummingbird feeders and accessories come in a wide price range.
*   **$5 - $15:** This range typically covers accessories like replacement bee guards or repellent sprays and pastes.
*   **$15 - $25:** You can find excellent, functional plastic feeders in this range, often in two-packs. They usually include basic features like ant moats.
*   **$25 - $50+:** This is the price range for high-quality glass feeders, decorative models, and feeders with advanced bee-proofing technology. They offer better durability and aesthetic appeal.
Setting a budget helps narrow your choices, but remember that investing a bit more in a durable, easy-to-clean feeder can save you money and hassle in the long run.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
When comparing models, focus on these key bee-proofing features:
*   **Bee Guards:** These are small plastic or metal cages or grates that fit over the feeding ports. They create a barrier that prevents bees from getting to the nectar.
*   **Saucer Design:** In saucer-style feeders, the nectar is held in a shallow dish, and the ports are on the top surface. This keeps the nectar level naturally out of reach for bees.
*   **Ant Moat:** This is a small cup or reservoir, usually located at the top of the feeder, that you fill with water. It creates a moat that ants cannot cross to get to the nectar.
*   **Leak-Proof Construction:** Dripping nectar is the number one attractant for all insects. Look for feeders with high-quality gaskets and a design that minimizes the chance of leaks.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The material of your feeder affects its longevity and ease of maintenance.
*   **Glass:** Glass feeders are durable, do not discolor from the sun, and are easy to clean thoroughly. However, they can break if dropped.
*   **Plastic:** Plastic feeders are lightweight and shatterproof, but they can become brittle and discolor over time with sun exposure. Look for high-quality, UV-stabilized plastic.
*   **Metal Components:** Check that any metal parts, like perches or hanging hooks, are rust-proof.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Perky-Pet and FEED GARDEN have years of experience designing and refining bird feeders. Choosing a product from a reputable brand often means you are getting a well-tested design, better customer support, and easier access to replacement parts like bee guards or gaskets.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
The capacity of the feeder is an important consideration. A large-capacity feeder (16 oz or more) requires less frequent refilling, which is convenient. However, in hot weather, nectar can spoil in just a couple of days. If you do not have a large number of hummingbirds, a smaller feeder (8-12 oz) might be better to ensure the nectar is always fresh. Also consider the weight of a full feeder, especially a large glass one, and ensure your hanging location can support it.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose hummingbird feeders to keep bees away?

Choosing the best hummingbird feeder to keep bees away involves a strategic approach. Following a few simple steps and keeping some professional tips in mind will help you find the perfect solution for your yard.

**Step-by-Step Selection Guide:**
1.  **Identify Your Main Pest:** First, determine if your primary issue is with bees, wasps, ants, or all of them. This will guide whether you prioritize bee guards, an ant moat, or a feeder that has both.
2.  **Choose a Feeder Style:** Decide between a traditional bottle feeder or a modern saucer feeder. Saucer feeders are often naturally more bee-resistant and easier to clean. Bottle feeders can hold more nectar but require good bee guards to be effective.
3.  **Select the Right Material:** Choose between glass and plastic. Glass offers durability and clarity but is breakable. High-quality plastic is unbreakable and lightweight but may degrade over time.
4.  **Check for Ease of Cleaning:** A feeder that is difficult to clean will not be used consistently. Look for models with a wide mouth and parts that fully disassemble. A clean feeder is crucial for hummingbird health.
5.  **Consider Capacity:** Choose a size appropriate for the number of hummingbirds you have. It is better to refill a smaller feeder with fresh nectar every few days than to let nectar spoil in a large, half-empty one.

**Professional Tips:**
*   **Placement is Key:** Hang your feeder in a location that is not directly next to bee-attracting flowers like lavender or bee balm. A spot with morning sun and afternoon shade is ideal to keep nectar fresh.
*   **Maintain the Right Nectar Ratio:** Always use a 4:1 ratio of water to plain white sugar. Never use honey, brown sugar, or red dye, as these can be harmful to hummingbirds and more attractive to bees.
*   **Keep it Clean:** Clean your feeder every 2-3 days in hot weather and at least once a week in cooler weather. Use a solution of hot water and a little vinegar, and rinse thoroughly.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Using Greasy Repellents:** Never apply oil, petroleum jelly, or other sticky substances to the feeder or pole. This can get on a bird's feathers, making it impossible for them to fly or stay warm.
*   **Ignoring Leaks:** A dripping feeder is an open invitation to insects. If your feeder starts to leak, replace the gasket or the entire feeder immediately.
