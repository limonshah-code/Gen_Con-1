## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Electric Canner Pressure Cooker

Choosing the right canner is a significant decision for any home preservation enthusiast. To ensure you select a model that fits your needs perfectly, consider these key factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess what you plan to can and how often. If you'll be preserving low-acid foods like green beans, corn, meats, or fish, a pressure canner is non-negotiable for safety. If your focus is on high-acid foods like fruits, jams, and pickles, a boiling water bath canner will suffice. Many pressure canners, both electric and stovetop, can also perform water bath canning, offering greater versatility. Consider your batch size. A small 9.5-quart model like the CAREY is great for small batches, while a 23-quart Presto is essential for processing a large garden harvest.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Your budget will play a significant role. Traditional stovetop pressure canners typically range from $100 to $200 and are known for their durability. Electric models, with their advanced automation and digital controls, generally fall in the $150 to $350 range. The canner's material affects its performance and longevity. Heavy-gauge aluminum is a popular choice for stovetop models because it's an excellent heat conductor, leading to fast, even heating. However, it can be prone to staining. Stainless steel, often found in electric models, is more durable, less reactive to acidic foods, and easier to clean, though it may be heavier and take longer to heat up. Check for sturdy handles and a well-fitting lid, as these are indicators of solid construction.

Maintenance is straightforward. After each use, wash the removable inner pot, sealing ring, and lid with warm, soapy water. Never immerse the main unit in water; wipe it clean with a damp cloth. **What's the difference between a pressure cooker and a pressure canner?**
A pressure cooker is designed to cook food quickly. A pressure canner is designed to process food for long-term storage. No, you cannot safely use a standard electric pressure cooker or multi-cooker for pressure canning. These devices are not designed to reach and maintain the exact, consistent temperatures and pressures required by USDA guidelines to safely preserve low-acid foods. Using one for canning poses a serious risk of botulism. Always use a dedicated pressure canner.
