First, assess your home environment. Do you have mostly hardwood floors, plush carpets, or a mix of both? A multi-level house might benefit from a lightweight lift-off model or a robot with multi-floor mapping. If you have a lot of low-profile furniture, a slim robotic vacuum is essential. For pet owners, features like tangle-free brushes and HEPA filters are non-negotiable.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($150-$600 ranges)
Your budget will significantly influence your options. Entry-level robotic vacuums with basic navigation can be found for around $150-$250. Mid-range models with better navigation (like Gyroscope) and app control typically fall in the $250-$400 range. High-end robotic vacuums with advanced features like LiDAR mapping, self-emptying bases, and powerful suction can cost anywhere from $400 to $600 or more. ### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for these key features when comparing models:
*   **Suction Power (measured in Pa):** For homes with pets and carpets, higher suction (1500Pa or more) is crucial for pulling up embedded hair and dander.
*   **Navigation Technology:** Basic robots use random paths. Gyroscope navigation offers a more methodical, S-shaped path. LiDAR (laser navigation) is the most advanced, creating precise maps for efficient, customized cleaning.
*   **Filtration:** A HEPA (High-Efficiency Particulate Air) filter is essential for pet owners and allergy sufferers, as it traps tiny particles like pollen, dander, and dust mites.
*   **Battery Life/Runtime:** Look for a runtime of at least 90-100 minutes to ensure the vacuum can clean a significant area on a single charge.
*   **Dustbin/Water Tank Capacity:** Larger capacities mean less frequent emptying and refilling, adding to the convenience.

A well-built vacuum will last for years. Look for models made from sturdy plastics and high-quality components. Check reviews for comments on longevity and common points of failure. The brush roll is a key component; tangle-free designs made with a combination of rubber fins and bristles tend to be more durable and effective for pet hair. The quality of the wheels also matters, especially for transitioning between different floor types and clearing thresholds.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Stick with reputable brands like ILIFE, Bissell, and Lefant, which have a proven track record of producing reliable cleaning appliances. ### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Consider the physical footprint of the device. Robotic vacuums are generally compact and come with a small charging dock. A lightweight, cordless model or an upright with a lift-off pod offers greater portability for whole-home cleaning.
