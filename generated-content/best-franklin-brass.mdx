## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Franklin Brass

Choosing the right cabinet hardware might seem like a small detail, but it has a huge impact on the final look and feel of your space. Here’s a guide to help you select the perfect Franklin Brass products for your needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess where and how the hardware will be used. Are you outfitting a high-traffic kitchen, a humid bathroom, or a decorative piece of furniture? For kitchens and bathrooms, consider durable, easy-to-clean finishes and even antimicrobial options. Measure the existing hole spacing (center-to-center) on your cabinets or drawers if you are replacing hardware. If you have non-standard spacing, a product like the Franklin Brass Adjusta-Pull can be a lifesaver. Match the scale of the hardware to the door or drawer; large pulls on small drawers can look out of place, and vice-versa.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($15-$80)
Franklin Brass offers a range of products to fit various budgets. Knobs are generally less expensive than pulls. You can expect to pay anywhere from $15-$30 for a 5-pack or 10-pack of basic knobs or pulls. Mid-range options, often with more intricate designs or popular finishes like Champagne Bronze, might fall in the $30-$50 range for a multi-pack. Premium products, such as those with antimicrobial coatings or specialized bathroom fixtures, can range from $50 to $80 or more. Buying in bulk (10-packs or more) almost always offers a better per-piece price.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Pay close attention to the technical details.
*   **Center-to-Center Measurement:** This is the distance between the center of the two screw holes on a pull. It's the most critical measurement for replacements. Common sizes are 3", 96mm, 4", and 128mm (5-1/16").
*   **Overall Length:** The full end-to-end length of a pull.
*   **Projection:** How far the knob or pull sticks out from the cabinet face. This is important for ensuring clearance and comfortable use.
*   **Material:** Most Franklin Brass hardware is made of durable metal like zinc, aluminum, or brass, ensuring longevity.
*   **Finish:** Choose a finish that complements your cabinetry, countertops, and appliances. Popular options include Satin Nickel, Matte Black, and Champagne Bronze.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for hardware made from solid metal rather than hollow or plated plastic. A good quality piece will feel substantial and heavy in your hand. Check for features like fade, scratch, and corrosion-resistant finishes. These are especially important in kitchens where hardware is exposed to moisture, oils, and frequent cleaning, and in bathrooms where humidity is high. A limited lifetime warranty is also a great indicator of a manufacturer's confidence in their product's durability.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Choosing a reputable brand like Franklin Brass, which has been trusted since 1946, provides a level of assurance. Established manufacturers are more likely to have consistent quality control, meaning the finish and size will be uniform across all pieces you buy. They also typically offer better customer support and warranties, protecting your investment.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While cabinet hardware isn't portable, its size is a crucial consideration. Ensure the projection of the knobs or pulls won't interfere with the opening of adjacent cabinets, doors, or appliances, especially in corners. Consider the scale of your room and furniture. Delicate knobs might get lost on large, heavy drawers, while oversized pulls can overwhelm a small vanity. It's often helpful to buy a single sample piece to test in your space before committing to a full set.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose franklin brass?

Choosing the perfect Franklin Brass hardware involves a blend of practical measurement and stylistic preference. Following a step-by-step process can help you make a confident choice.

1.  **Define Your Style:** First, determine the overall aesthetic you want to achieve. Are you aiming for modern and sleek, traditional and classic, or a rustic farmhouse look? Browse inspiration photos to see what hardware is used with cabinetry similar to yours. Franklin Brass offers distinct collections like the modern Maxted or the versatile Francisco to suit different tastes.

2.  **Measure Everything:** If you are replacing existing hardware, measure the center-to-center distance on your current pulls. This will be your primary guide. If you are drilling new holes, you have more freedom. A good rule of thumb is to use pulls that are roughly 1/3 the length of the drawer. For doors, consider using knobs or smaller pulls. Don't forget to measure the thickness of your doors and drawers to ensure the included screws will be the right length.

3.  **Choose Your Finish:** Select a finish that complements other elements in the room, such as your faucet, lighting, and appliances. Franklin Brass makes this easy by designing their finishes, like Champagne Bronze, to coordinate with major plumbing brands like Delta. Don't be afraid to mix metals, but try to keep it to two or three finishes for a cohesive look.

4.  **Test a Sample:** Before buying hardware for your entire kitchen, purchase a single knob and a single pull in your chosen style and finish. Install them and live with them for a few days. See how they look in different lights, how they feel in your hand, and how they function. This small step can prevent a costly mistake.

5.  **Avoid Common Mistakes:** A frequent error is choosing hardware that is out of scale—either too large or too small for the cabinetry. Another is overlooking the projection, which can lead to pulls that are difficult to grip or that bump into adjacent surfaces. Finally, don't sacrifice quality for a slightly lower price. A durable finish and solid construction are worth the modest extra investment.
