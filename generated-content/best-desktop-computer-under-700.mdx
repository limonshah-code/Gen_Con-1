## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Desktop Computer Under 700

Navigating the market for a budget-friendly desktop can be tricky. To ensure you get the best value and a machine that meets your needs, consider these crucial factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, define what you'll be using the computer for. Is it for general web browsing and office work? Are you a student who needs it for research and writing? Or are you an aspiring gamer looking to play popular titles? A PC for basic tasks can prioritize RAM and a fast SSD, while a gaming PC will need a dedicated graphics card (GPU). Ensure the PC has the necessary ports (USB, HDMI, DisplayPort) for your monitors and accessories.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
While our focus is under $700, there's a lot of variation within this range.
-   **$300-$450:** Expect reliable renewed office PCs (like Dell Optiplex models) with older i5/i7 processors, 8-16GB RAM, and an SSD. These are excellent for general use and office work.
-   **$450-$700:** In this range, you can find more powerful renewed machines with more RAM/storage, entry-level gaming PCs with dedicated GPUs (like the GTX 1660 or RX 550), or even new desktops with modern, efficient processors.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
-   **Processor (CPU):** Look for an Intel Core i5/i7 or an AMD Ryzen 5/7. For newer models, an Intel Core Ultra 5 is excellent. Pay attention to the generation; a newer i5 can outperform an older i7.
-   **RAM (Memory):** 16GB is the sweet spot for this price range. It provides smooth multitasking for both productivity and gaming. 32GB is a fantastic bonus if you can find it.
-   **Storage (SSD/HDD):** An SSD (Solid State Drive) is a must-for the operating system. It makes the entire computer feel dramatically faster. Aim for at least 256GB, with 512GB or 1TB being ideal. Some PCs offer a smaller SSD for speed and a larger HDD for mass storage.
-   **Graphics Card (GPU):** If you plan to game, a dedicated GPU is essential. In this budget, look for cards like the NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3060, GTX 1660 Ti, or AMD Radeon RX series. For non-gamers, integrated graphics are perfectly fine.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Business-grade desktops from brands like Dell (Optiplex) and Lenovo (IdeaCentre) are built to last, even when refurbished. They often feature sturdy metal cases and high-quality internal components designed for long-term reliability. For gaming PCs, check for good airflow and multiple fans to ensure components stay cool under pressure.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with reputable brands like Dell, Lenovo, HP, and specialized gaming builders like ViprTech or STGAubron is a safe bet. These companies have established track records for quality and customer support. When buying renewed, ensure it’s from a Microsoft Authorized Refurbisher for an added layer of quality assurance.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Desktop computers come in various sizes.
-   **Towers:** Offer the most space for components and upgrades.
-   **Small Form Factor (SFF):** A great compromise, offering decent power in a compact case that can fit on or under a desk easily.
-   **Slim Desktops:** The most space-saving option, perfect for minimalist setups or tight spaces, but with limited upgrade potential.
Consider your available desk space before choosing a size.

Investing in a desktop computer under $700 is an incredibly wise decision for a vast majority of users. This price point represents the sweet spot where value and performance intersect. You are no longer limited to sluggish, basic machines. ## What Is the Best Way to Choose deskcomputer under 700?

Choosing the best desktop computer under $700 requires a methodical approach to ensure you get the most value for your money. Follow these steps to make an informed decision.

1.  **Define Your Primary Use Case:** Be honest about what you'll do 90% of the time. Is it for office work (spreadsheets, email), school (research, writing), content consumption (streaming, browsing), or gaming? This will dictate whether you need a powerful CPU, lots of RAM, or a dedicated GPU.
2.  **Prioritize Key Components:** For this budget, an SSD is non-negotiable for system responsiveness. After that, prioritize based on your use case.
    *   **Office/School:** Prioritize CPU (i5/Ryzen 5 or better) and RAM (16GB+).
    *   **Gaming:** The GPU is king. Look for the best graphics card you can find (e.g., RTX 3060, GTX 1660 Ti).
    *   **General Use:** A balanced system with an i5/Ryzen 5, 16GB RAM, and a 512GB SSD is ideal.
3.  **Consider New vs. Renewed:** Don't overlook professionally refurbished business desktops. They often provide incredible value, offering high-end components (like i7 CPUs and 32GB RAM) for the price of a new entry-level machine. Ensure they come from a reputable seller with a good warranty (90 days is standard).
4.  **Check Connectivity:** Make sure the PC has all the ports you need. Count the number of USB ports, and check for video outputs (HDMI, DisplayPort) that match your monitor(s). Built-in Wi-Fi and Bluetooth are also significant convenience factors.
5.  **Read Reviews and Watch Videos:** Once you've narrowed it down to a few models, look for reviews from both professional critics and actual users. User reviews can provide insight into long-term reliability and customer service experiences.

**Common Mistake to Avoid:** A common mistake is getting fixated on one specification while ignoring others. A powerful CPU with only 8GB of RAM and a slow hard drive will feel sluggish. Look for a balanced system where the components complement each other.

Within the under $700 budget, the $500-$650 range typically offers the best value. Spending less than $400 is fine for very basic needs, but the performance jump in the higher bracket is significant.

**What features are most important in deskcomputer under 700?**
The three most important features are the processor (CPU), RAM, and storage type. Aim for at least an Intel Core i5 or AMD Ryzen 5 processor, 16GB of RAM for smooth multitasking, and a Solid State Drive (SSD) for fast boot times and overall system responsiveness. For gaming, a dedicated graphics card (GPU) becomes the most critical component.

**How do I maintain my deskcomputer under 700?**
Maintenance is simple. Keep the operating system and drivers updated to ensure security and performance. Physically, keep the computer in a well-ventilated area to prevent overheating. About once every six months, power it down, unplug it, and use compressed air to gently blow dust out of the vents and fans to maintain good airflow.

**Are expensive deskcomputer under 700 worth it?**
It depends on your needs. For professional gamers, video editors, or engineers, a more expensive PC with a top-of-the-line CPU and GPU is a necessary tool. However, for the vast majority of users, including students, office workers, and casual gamers, a desktop under $700 provides more than enough power. The law of diminishing returns is strong with PCs; the performance gain from spending more is often not worth the cost for average users.

**What brands make the best deskcomputer under 700?**
Dell and Lenovo are leaders in this category, especially with their reliable and high-value Optiplex and IdeaCentre business lines, which are fantastic when purchased renewed. **How long do deskcomputer under 700 typically last?**
A well-chosen desktop in this price range can easily last 5-7 years for general productivity tasks. Its useful lifespan for gaming might be shorter, around 3-4 years, before it starts struggling with the latest titles. Because desktops are upgradeable, you can often extend their life by adding more RAM or a new graphics card down the road.

**Can I find good deskcomputer under 700 on a budget?**
Absolutely. The under $700 category is one of the best for finding high-value computers. By focusing on professionally refurbished business-class machines or value-focused gaming prebuilts, you can get a powerful and reliable computer that will handle a wide range of tasks without breaking the bank.
