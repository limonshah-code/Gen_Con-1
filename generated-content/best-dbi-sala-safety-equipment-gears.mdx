### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, clearly define the work you will be doing. Are you in construction, roofing, tower climbing, or general industry? The demands of each job are different. Consider the entire fall protection system (Anchorage, Bodywear, Connector). Ensure your new gear is compatible with the other components you use.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($50-$500 ranges)
DBI-SALA equipment spans a wide price range. A basic, compliant harness might cost around $100, while a premium, feature-rich harness like the ExoFit NEX could be upwards of $400. ### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that enhance safety and usability. Examine the build quality. Look for tight, consistent stitching on the webbing. The hardware (D-rings, buckles) should be forged or stamped from high-quality steel or aluminum and feel substantial. ### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Consider how you will transport and store your gear. If you move between job sites frequently, a lightweight harness and a compact SRL might be preferable. Proper storage is a key part of equipment maintenance and longevity.
