## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Door Knobs With Locks

Choosing the right door knob involves more than just picking a style you like. It's a key component of your home's security. Here’s what to consider to ensure you make the best choice.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, determine the knob's function. Is it for an exterior door requiring high security (keyed entry), or an interior bedroom or bathroom needing privacy (privacy knob)? Check your door's specifications: measure the backset (the distance from the door's edge to the center of the hole for the knob) and the door's thickness. Most knobs have adjustable latches, but it's always best to confirm compatibility.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Door knobs with locks can range widely in price. Basic, functional knobs can be found for as little as $15-$25. Mid-range options with better materials and features like re-keying technology typically fall in the $30-$60 range. High-end or designer knobs with superior security features and premium finishes can cost $60 or more. Set a budget based on your security needs and the number of knobs you need to purchase.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for an ANSI (American National Standards Institute) Grade. Grade 3 is standard for residential use, Grade 2 is better, and Grade 1 is the highest level of security, typically for commercial use. Features like Kwikset's SmartKey allow for easy re-keying. Anti-bump or anti-pick cylinders, like the one in the CML knob, offer enhanced protection against tampering.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The material of the knob is crucial for its longevity. Solid metals like stainless steel, brass, or zinc alloy are far superior to knobs with plastic internal components. The finish (e.g., satin nickel, polished brass, matte black) should be corrosion-resistant, especially for exterior doors exposed to the elements. A solid, heavy feel is often a good indicator of quality construction.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Established brands like Kwikset, Schlage, and others have a long history of producing reliable hardware. They often back their products with strong warranties, such as lifetime mechanical and finish warranties, which can be a sign of quality and provide you with long-term peace of mind. Reading reviews and choosing a trusted brand can save you from the frustration of a failing lock.

### Installation & Fit - Considerations for space and storage
While portability isn't a factor, installation and fit are critical. Most modern door knobs are designed for easy DIY installation with just a screwdriver. Ensure the knob you choose comes with clear instructions and all necessary hardware. Check that the latch and strike plate will fit your door's existing cutouts to avoid having to make modifications to the door or frame.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose door knobs with locks?

Following a structured approach can help you select the perfect door knob with a lock for your needs.

**1. Identify the Door's Purpose:**
First, determine the type of lock you need.
- **Exterior Doors (Front, Back, Garage):** Require a keyed entry knob for high security.
- **Interior Doors (Bedroom, Bathroom):** A privacy knob with an internal lock is sufficient.
- **Closets/Hallways:** A non-locking passage knob is all that's needed.

**2. Measure Your Door:**
Before you shop, take two critical measurements:
- **Backset:** The distance from the edge of the door to the center of the borehole. This is typically 2-3/8" or 2-3/4". Most new knobs have an adjustable latch to fit both.
- **Door Thickness:** Most interior and exterior doors are 1-3/8" to 1-3/4" thick. Confirm your door falls within the range specified by the knob manufacturer.

**3. Define Your Security Needs:**
For exterior doors, consider the level of security you need. An ANSI Grade 2 or 3 is suitable for most homes. For enhanced protection, look for features like anti-bump cylinders or smart re-keying technology.

**4. Choose Your Style and Finish:**
Select a style (round, lever, etc.) and finish (satin nickel, matte black, etc.) that complements your home's decor. Consistency in hardware style throughout your home creates a cohesive look.

**5. Avoid Common Mistakes:**
- **Ignoring ANSI Grade:** Don't just buy based on looks; a certified lock provides proven security.
- **Forgetting Handing:** Some lever-style knobs are right-handed or left-handed. Most modern knobs are reversible, but it's always good to check.
- **Buying a Single Key for Everything:** While convenient, using the same key for every door can be a security risk if that one key is lost.
