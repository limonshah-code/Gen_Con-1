## What to Look for Before Buying the Best High Gloss Tile Sealer

Choosing the right sealer involves more than just picking the shiniest bottle on the shelf. To ensure you get the best results for your specific project, consider these crucial factors.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
The most important factor is the type of tile you have. Porous tiles like Saltillo, slate, or unglazed ceramic absorb sealer well. Non-porous tiles like porcelain or glazed ceramic require a specialized sealer, like a two-part system, that can bond to the surface. Also, consider the location. An outdoor patio needs a UV-stable, weather-resistant sealer, while a bathroom floor might benefit from one with added slip resistance. High-traffic areas like hallways and kitchens demand a more durable, abrasion-resistant formula.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
High gloss tile sealers can range in price from around $25 for a basic quart to over $100 for a professional-grade, two-part kit. A standard, good-quality water-based sealer will typically cost between $30-$50 per quart. While it can be tempting to go for the cheapest option, investing in a more durable sealer like CoverTec's GlazeGuard can save you money in the long run by significantly extending the time between reapplications. Set a budget, but prioritize longevity and suitability for your tile type.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose high gloss tile sealer?

Choosing the right high gloss tile sealer requires a methodical approach to ensure you get a product that performs well and lasts. Follow this step-by-step guide for the best results.

**1. Identify Your Surface:** First, determine the exact type of tile you have. Is it porous natural stone like slate or travertine? Is it highly porous clay like Saltillo? Or is it dense and non-porous like porcelain or glazed ceramic? This is the single most important step, as a sealer for porous tile will not work on a glazed surface.

**2. Assess the Environment:** Consider the location of the tile. Is it a high-traffic hallway, a wet bathroom, a greasy kitchen, or an outdoor patio exposed to sun and rain? Your choice will depend on these conditions. You'll need high durability for traffic, moisture resistance for bathrooms, and UV stability for outdoors.

**3. Define Your Aesthetic Goal:** While you're looking for a high gloss, some sealers also enhance or deepen the color of the tile. Products like STONETECH's sealer are specifically designed for this. Decide if you want a clear coat that just adds shine or one that also enriches the color.

**4. Check for Safety and VOCs:** For interior projects, especially if you have children, pets, or respiratory sensitivities, a low-VOC, water-based sealer is the best choice. They have minimal odor and are safer to work with in enclosed spaces.

**5. Read Reviews and Follow Instructions:** Once you've narrowed down your options, read recent user reviews for real-world feedback. When you've made your purchase, read the manufacturer's instructions carefully. Proper surface preparation—cleaning and ensuring the tile is completely dry—is absolutely critical for the sealer to adhere correctly and last.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Applying to a Dirty or Damp Surface:** This is the number one cause of sealer failure, leading to a cloudy finish or peeling.
*   **Applying Too Thickly:** Most sealers work best in thin, even coats. A thick coat can take a very long time to cure and may remain tacky.
*   **Not Testing First:** Always test the sealer on a spare tile or in an inconspicuous area to ensure you like the look and that it adheres properly.
